How to Get Better Aim in Valorant

Aim isn't magic. It's a stack of habits you repeat until hitting heads is the only outcome. Use this routine to answer the classic question of how to get better aim in Valorant without spending five hours in the Range.

Warm Up With Intention

Spend ten minutes jogging through the Range on hard mode. Focus on three cues: crosshair height, micro-adjustments, and recoil control. Stop the moment your mind drifts; intentional reps beat volume.

Layer Movement + Aim

Load a custom deathmatch and challenge yourself to only shoot after a clean counter-strafe. Say "stop, shoot, strafe" out loud if it helps. This links good movement to every bullet fired.

Record Two Duels Per Match

Clip one win and one loss. Review them slowly and note where your crosshair sat before contact, whether you jiggle peeked, and how fast you corrected after missing. You'll instantly see the patterns that hold back your rank.

Run Micro Drills

Turn every weakness into a five-minute drill: grind close-range flicks, strafe around a box with only burst fire, or practice ADS tap bursts on long sightlines. Micro drills make improvements stick.

Protect Your Mindset

Aim progress dies when tilt hits. Set a rule: if you lose three duels to the same mistake, hop back into the Range for five minutes and reset. Treat aim like a system, not a mood.
